What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile phone) when you visit a website. They allow the website to recognize your device and remember certain information about your preferences or actions.

Cookies can be classified in several ways:

  • First-party cookies: Set by MediSync directly
  • Third-party cookies: Set by external services integrated into MediSync
  • Session cookies: Temporary cookies that expire when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies: Cookies that remain on your device for a set period or until manually deleted

Why We Use Cookies

MediSync uses cookies and similar technologies for several essential purposes:

  • Authentication: Verifying your identity and maintaining your logged-in session
  • Security: Protecting against unauthorized access and fraudulent activity
  • Functionality: Remembering your preferences and settings
  • Performance: Analyzing platform usage to improve speed and reliability
  • Analytics: Understanding how users interact with MediSync to enhance usability

Session Storage & Local Storage

In addition to cookies, MediSync uses browser storage mechanisms to enhance functionality:

Session Storage

Temporary data storage that is cleared when you close your browser tab. Used for:

  • Preserving form data during multi-step workflows
  • Caching frequently accessed inventory data to reduce server load
  • Maintaining state during navigation within the platform

Local Storage

Persistent storage that remains until manually cleared. Used for:

  • Offline capability and data synchronization
  • User interface preferences and customizations
  • Draft content (e.g., unsent RFS requests, partially completed forms)

Managing Cookies

Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • View which cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete existing cookies
  • Block all cookies or specific types of cookies
  • Set preferences for cookie acceptance

Please refer to your browser's help documentation for specific instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

Impact of Blocking Cookies

Warning: Disabling or blocking cookies, particularly strictly necessary cookies, will significantly impair MediSync functionality:

  • You will not be able to log in or maintain an authenticated session
  • Your preferences and settings will not be saved
  • Platform performance may be degraded
  • Some features may not function correctly

If you choose to disable cookies and encounter issues, you will need to re-enable them to use MediSync.

Do Not Track (DNT)

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) signal that requests websites not to track user activity. Currently, there is no industry consensus on how to respond to DNT signals.

MediSync does not respond to DNT signals because:

  • We do not use cookies for advertising or cross-site tracking
  • Most cookies we use are essential for platform functionality
  • Our analytics are focused on platform improvement, not user profiling

Data Retention

  • Session cookies are automatically removed when you close your browser
  • Persistent cookies expire after their designated lifespan
  • You can manually delete cookies at any time through browser settings
  • Clearing browser data will remove all locally stored cookies and storage
